## 4.2 Repair Prediction Methodology

Corrosion growth rates are determined as follows:

  * **Matched defects**: CGR = max(0, (depth_run2 − depth_run1) / years_between). Negative growth is clamped to zero (corrosion does not physically shrink; apparent reduction is attributed to tool measurement variability).
  * **Unmatched run-2 defects**: features reported only in the later run are assumed to have been below the {{POD_PCT}}% WT probability-of-detection threshold at the earlier run. The CGR is then computed assuming depth_old = {{POD_PCT}}% × WT, which is the most conservative non-zero growth rate consistent with the tool's detection limit.

To capture the population-level upper bound on growth, the 95th percentile (P95) of the feature-specific CGRs is computed separately for internal and external defects. In **{{CGR_MODE}}** mode, every defect's used growth rate is `max(feature_cgr, surface_P95)` — features that happen to grow slowly are not allowed to escape the population-level expectation.

The repair-year prediction simulates each defect's depth forward year by year, recomputing Psafe/ERF at each step. Two stop conditions are checked, whichever fires first:

  * depth ≥ 80% WT (depth-only repair criterion), OR
  * ERF ≥ 1.0 (insufficient pressure capacity).

The year at which either trigger first fires is the predicted repair year. If neither fires within the {{HORIZON_YEARS}}-year horizon ending {{HORIZON_END_DATE}}, the defect is reported as "no action required within horizon."

## 4.3 Response to Pipeline ILI Results (API 1160 Categories)

Per API 1160 §11.5, metal-loss findings are placed into one of three response categories based on their predicted time-to-repair:

## 4.4 Recommended Repair Methods

For features requiring repair, the operator's standard repair methods include:

  * **Type-A composite sleeve (e.g. Clock Spring, NRI, ARMOR plate)** for defects with depth less than 80% WT and no through-wall risk. The composite is designed to a hoop stress of MAOP and laminated over the defect after surface preparation.
  * **Type-B steel sleeve** for through-wall or near-through-wall defects. The sleeve is welded to the parent pipe and provides pressure containment independent of the underlying material.
  * **Cut-and-replace** for short pipe segments with multiple severe defects or where the repair would interfere with girth welds.
  * **Hot-tap sleeve with welded saddle** where the line cannot be depressurised.

The selection of the repair method for each feature is the operator's call, informed by site access, depressurisation feasibility, and the local pipe support / coating condition.
